The first game in the series, Sonic the Hedgehog, was released for the Sega Genesis console in 1991 and introduced the basic gameplay mechanics that would define the series. The game is a 2D side-scrolling platformer that challenges the player to control Sonic as he speeds through seven zones, each with its own theme, enemies, obstacles and boss. The game also features a special stage that can be accessed by collecting 50 rings and jumping into a giant ring at the end of each level. In the special stage, Sonic has to navigate a rotating maze and collect six Chaos Emeralds, which are powerful gems that play a key role in the series’ lore.

The game was praised for its colorful graphics, catchy music, smooth animation and fast-paced gameplay that gave the player a sense of speed and freedom. The game also established Sonic as a charismatic and cool character with a rebellious attitude and a love for adventure. The game was a huge success and sold over 15 million copies worldwide, making it the best-selling Sega Genesis game ever. The game also spawned several sequels, spin-offs, adaptations and merchandise that expanded the Sonic universe and its characters.

The game also featured a multiplayer mode where two players can compete or cooperate as Sonic and Tails. The game was even more successful than the first one and sold over 18 million copies worldwide.

The game continued the story from Sonic 2 and introduced Knuckles, a red echidna who guards the Master Emerald, a powerful gem that controls the Chaos Emeralds. Knuckles initially acts as an antagonist who is tricked by Dr. Eggman into believing that Sonic is his enemy, but later joins forces with him to stop Eggman’s plans. The game also featured a save system, a more complex level design, new power-ups and special stages that can be accessed by finding giant rings hidden in each level.
