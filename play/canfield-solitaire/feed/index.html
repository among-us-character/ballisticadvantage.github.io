Spider Solitaire Rules

  • Shuffle the deck and deal ten piles of cards face down, with one card face up on each pile.
  • You can move face-up cards to other piles, regardless of suit, as long as the move creates a descending sequence.
  • You can also move sequences of cards from one pile to another, as long as the sequence is in descending order and all of the cards are the same suit.
  • If you have a king, you can move it to an empty pile.
  • You can deal a new row of ten cards face up, one card on each pile, whenever you run out of moves. However, you must fill all of the empty piles before you can deal a new row of cards.
  • The game is won when you have built eight stacks of cards in descending order, all of the same suit.

Spider Solitaire Strategies

Here are some advanced strategies that can help you win more games of Spider Solitaire:

  • Plan ahead. When you are moving cards, try to think about how your moves will affect the rest of the board. Look for opportunities to create new sequences and open up empty piles.
  • Be patient. It is often better to wait for the right opportunity to move a card than to make a hasty move that could make the situation worse.
  • Don’t be afraid to deal a new row of cards. If you are stuck, dealing a new row of cards can give you the fresh start you need. However, be sure to fill all of the empty piles before you deal a new row of cards.

How To play Klondike Solitaire:

  1. Shuffle the deck and deal 28 cards face down to the tableau, arranged in seven piles of cards, with one card face up on each pile. The remaining 24 cards are placed face down in a stack called the stock.
  2. The object of the game is to move all of the cards from the tableau and stock to the four foundation piles, which are stacked in ascending suit order (ace through king).
  3. To move a card, you can either:
    • Place it on a foundation pile if it is the next card in the sequence.
    • Place it on another tableau pile if it is one rank lower than the top card and the opposite color.
  4. If you cannot move any cards, you can flip over the top card of the stock pile and add it to the tableau. You can continue flipping over cards from the stock pile until you find a card that you can move, or until the stock pile is empty.
  5. Once you have moved all of the cards from the tableau and stock to the foundation piles, you have won the game.

Tips for playing Klondike Solitaire:

  • Try to keep as many tableau piles as possible open. This will give you more options for moving cards.
