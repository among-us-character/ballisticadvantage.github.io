How to Play Solitaire Grande

Solitaire Grande is a variant of the solitaire card game. Here are some details about Solitaire Grande:

• It is played with a 52 card deck and the aim is to build 4 stacks of cards in suit from Ace to King.

• To start, 14 cards are dealt out onto 7 columns in an arrangement. The rest of the cards form the stock or draw pile.

• Cards can be moved to build stacks based on suit and alternating color. An Ace can be placed on a King of the same suit.

• Cards from the draw pile can be added to the tableau if there is an available space.

• When no more moves are possible, cards from the stacks can be transferred to the waste pile to make new moves available.

• The game is won when all 52 cards are in 4 stacks of Ace to King in suit. If no more moves are possible before all cards are stacked, the game is lost.

• Solitaire Grande is considered a more difficult variant of solitaire due to the limited number of moves and strict stacking rules. Strategy and planning is important.

So in summary, Solitaire Grande is a certain version or variant of the solitaire card game that is more challenging than the basic solitaire game. It has an objective of building 4 suit stacks from Ace to King and specific rules around card placement.
