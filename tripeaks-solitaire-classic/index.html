Shuffle the deck and lay out seven piles of cards, with the first pile containing one card, the second containing two cards, the third containing three cards, and so on. The remaining cards are placed in a separate pile, known as the stockpile.

Step 2: Objective

The objective of Solitaire is to move all the cards to their foundation piles, which are located at the top right of the game board. Each foundation pile should start with an Ace and end with a King. Cards can be moved to the foundation piles in ascending order, according to their suit.

Step 3: Game Play

You will begin by flipping over the top card of the stockpile. You can then move cards from the tableau piles (the seven piles of cards you laid out earlier) to either the foundation piles or the waste pile (a separate pile where cards that cannot be played are placed).

Cards can be moved from one tableau pile to another in descending order, and cards can only be placed on top of a card that is one rank higher and of a different color. For example, a red seven can be placed on top of a black eight.

Step 4: Winning the Game

To win the game, you must move all the cards to the foundation piles. Keep in mind that you cannot move cards from the foundation piles back to the tableau piles.

Step 5: Tips and Tricks

Always try to move cards to the foundation piles first.

Move cards between tableau piles to create space for other cards.

Use the waste pile to your advantage. If you can’t play a card from the tableau piles, move it to the waste pile to free up space.

Use the undo button sparingly. While it can be tempting to use the undo button, doing so too often can disrupt the flow of the game.
